Project Overview

Phase 1 solved the foundation: trustworthy authentication and a scannable clinical command center.Post-launch data revealed two deeper problems that structural redesign alone could not fix: alert fatigue and sync failures were quietly weakening data confidence.

Phase 2 introduced intelligent alert management and complete sync transparency to reduce cognitive burden and restore clinical trust.

AI tool was integrated into the design process: Claude AI for research synthesis

Background

Follownig the Phase 1 launch, usage analytics and follow-up interviews surfaced two present pain points that better visual hierarchy alone couldn't address.

Persisting Problem Root Cause Why Phase 1 Couldn't Fully Solve It
Alert fatigue remained Volume problem — too many alerts, not a display problem Hierarchy helps scanning but cannot reduce noise at the source
Data trust issues Network instability in clinical environments is unpredictable Visual feedback confirms failure — it cannot prevent it

Problem Statement

Alert Fatigue Was Endemic

Critical notifications competed with routine updates, scattered across patient records and system notifications with no central management. Staff began ignoring notifications, including the things that mattered.

Invisible Sync Created Anxiety

Staff couldn't tell if changes were saved, leading to duplicate documentation, verification calls, and eroded trust in the system they depended on for every clinical decision.

Challenges Faced

# Challenge Critical Impact
01 Alerts scattered across multiple locations — no central hub High — staff wasting time hunting for notifications
02 No severity differentiation — Critical and routine had equal visual weight Critical — alert blindness developing
03 Acknowledging an alert required navigating away from current screen High — 15% extra context-switching clicks per shift
04 No filtering, deferral, or personalization of notifications Medium — staff had no control over noise volume

06 Silent sync failures went completely unnoticed until data was missing Critical — staff acting on stale clinical data

PROJECT REFLECTION

Phase 2 highlighted that "invisible" design—sync transparency, audit trails, and conflict resolution—rebuilds trust. Integrating AI tools like Claude and Dovetail demonstrated that AI's immediate value is in the design process, freeing designers for judgment-based work.

In healthcare, the most important design decision is often not what you build, it's what you make visible. A system staff can see and understand is a system staff can trust. And trust, in a clinical environment, is not a nice-to-have. It is the product.
