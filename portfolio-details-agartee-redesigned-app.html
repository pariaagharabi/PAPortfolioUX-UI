Project Overview

The redesign introduced a secure authentication system, a more informative and efficient dashboard, an organized and structured alerts ecosystem with filtering options, and real-time AWS sync feedback to help healthcares providers trust the system while managing time-sensitive patient data.

Background

Within the first year the app was adopted by multiple facilities, serving hundreds of patients and dozens of daily active nurses. While the core functionality was solid and well-received, continuous feedback and analytics revealed clear opportunities to evolve the experience for high-pressure, time-critical environments. As real teams began using it daily, the product revealed a clear next step: beyond simply digitizing records, it needed to actively support time-pressured clinical decision-making, reduce cognitive load for diverse staff (including older users), and build strong trust in real-time AWS-synced data.

Problem Statement

The initial version of the Patient Data Management application functioned well for basic record-keeping, but revealed usability gaps as daily usage grew.

Challenges Faced:

  1. The initial authentication screens felt dated and caused repeated login failures, especially for older staff members of staff.
  2. The dashboard did not surface key patient insights at a glance, forcing staff to navigate multiple screens during time-sensitive situations.
  3. Critical and informational alerts were buried inside patient records or scattered in notifications with no dedicated place to review them
  4. After saving changes, users never received clear confirmation that data had successfully synced to AWS

My Role

Research Approach

USER PAIN POINTS

Frequent Interruptions
Nurses face constant disruptions during shifts, leading to abandoned or incomplete data entries.
Error-Prone Manual Processes
Manual input of information is time-consuming and prone to human error.
Dynamic Work Environments
Vitals are recorded in varying locations like bedside or hallways, causing inconsistency and workflow challenges.
Delayed Critical Alerts
Critical changes in patient status may go unnoticed without immediate, attention-grabbing notifications.
Escaped Entries
Interrupted workflows leave half-completed entries that the system does not effectively flag or resume.
Inconsistent initial research
Early gaps in user research led to unclear requirements, scope creep, and delayed buy-in.
Verbal Dependency
Doctors rely on verbal updates or informal messaging when digital data is unavailable or outdated.
Scalability concerns
Rolling out the app across multiple facilities without proof of long-term efficiency raised adoption hesitancy.
Enable Real-Time Access
Legacy system integration bottlenecks limit interoperability and data flow, restricting real-time access and creating critical operational challenges.

Reflections and Learnings

This project provided valuable insights into the challenges of designing for high-stakes healthcare environments where accuracy, speed, and clarity are essential. Key takeaways include:

  1. Balancing complexity and simplicity: Designing an interface that consolidates critical patient information without overwhelming users required iterative refinement and close collaboration with clinical staff.
  2. Importance of user research: Direct shadowing and interviews with nurses and pharmacists were essential for uncovering hidden pain points and validating design decisions, ensuring the product truly meets user needs.
  3. Data-driven iteration: Continuous feedback loops and analytics allowed us to measure impact, prioritize features, and optimize workflows effectively, reinforcing the value of an agile, user-centered approach.
  4. Cross-disciplinary collaboration: Working closely with engineers, product managers, and healthcare professionals highlighted the need for clear communication and shared goals to translate complex requirements into intuitive solutions.
  5. Adaptability and empathy: This project underscored the necessity of understanding users’ stressful, time-sensitive contexts and designing with empathy to create tools that genuinely support their work.
