Project Overview

The redesign introduced a secure authentication system, a more informative and efficient dashboard, an organized and structured alerts ecosystem with filtering options, and real-time AWS sync feedback to help healthcares providers trust the system while managing time-sensitive patient data.

Background

Within the first year the app was adopted by multiple facilities, serving hundreds of patients and dozens of daily active nurses. While the core functionality was solid and well-received, continuous feedback and analytics revealed clear opportunities to evolve the experience for high-pressure, time-critical environments. As real teams began using it daily, the product revealed a clear next step: beyond simply digitizing records, it needed to actively support time-pressured clinical decision-making, reduce cognitive load for diverse staff (including older users), and build strong trust in real-time AWS-synced data.

Problem Statement

The initial version of the Patient Data Management application functioned well for basic record-keeping, but revealed usability gaps as daily usage grew.

Challenges Faced:

  1. The initial authentication screens felt dated and caused repeated login failures, especially for older staff members of staff.
  2. The dashboard did not surface key patient insights at a glance, forcing staff to navigate multiple screens during time-sensitive situations.
  3. Critical and informational alerts were buried inside patient records or scattered in notifications with no dedicated place to review them
  4. After saving changes, users never received clear confirmation that data had successfully synced to AWS

My Role

As the original designer of the Patient Data Management application, my role evolved from initial concept to leading this critical modernization initiative. This unique perspective allowed me to execute targeted, high-impact improvements

  • Conducted remote unmoderated usability tests and remote structured interviews
  • Delivered all flows, wireframes, high-fidelity screens, prototypes, and the updated design system in Figma
  • Redesigned the authentication system, created a structured alerts ecosystem with filtering options, and real-time AWS sync feedback (success/failure states)
  • Collaborated closely with the cross-functional team

01

Frictionless Secure Access

38% faster login · Zero failed attempts for trained staff

Redesigned the authentication flow end-to-end to meet enterprise security requirements without slowing clinical operations. The new design preserves user muscle memory and eliminates the error patterns that previously caused access delays through an accessibility-first approach.

Key Insight
Repeated login failures, especially among older staff, indicated that the previous layout and interaction patterns were causing avoidable support requests and access delays. The legacy interface averaged 2+ minutes per login (~132 seconds).

Challenge

Repeated login failures, especially among older staff. The dated, slow interface caused credential confusion and wasted critical time during shift changes. Login time averaged over 2 minutes (~132 seconds).

Solution

Introduced progressive disclosure, smart session persistence, and accessible input patterns. Error states now guide users to resolution rather than dead ends, leading directly to the 34% reduction in helpdesk volume.

Key Capabilities

Remember Device

Secure persistent sessions reduce daily logins by 70%.

02

Contextual Errors

Inline guidance prevents repeated mistakes.

03

Accessible Inputs

Large touch targets, high contrast, clear focus states.

Seamless shift changes without full re-authentication.

02

Command Center Redesign

40% faster patient lookup · Priority-driven hierarchy

Transformed a flat data display into an intelligent command center. Information architecture now mirrors clinical decision-making—surfacing what matters most during time-critical moments.

Key Insight
Critical patient data buried under routine information.

Challenge

Critical patient data buried under routine information. Staff navigated multiple screens to piece together a patient picture, losing precious seconds during emergencies.

Solution

Implemented tiered information hierarchy based on clinical urgency. Real-time vitals and alerts surface immediately while supporting data remains one interaction away.

04

AWS Sync Transparency

100% visibility · Zero silent failures

Introduced complete transparency into the AWS synchronization pipeline. Staff now have certainty about data state, with clear feedback for every save, sync, and conflict resolution.

Key Insight
Invisible sync operations created anxiety.

Challenge

Invisible sync operations created anxiety. Staff couldn't tell if changes were saved, leading to duplicate documentation, verification calls, and eroded trust in the system.

Solution

Designed explicit sync states with progress indicators, success confirmations, and graceful error handling.

Key Capabilities

02

04

Last Successful Sync Timestamp

A clear, always-visible indication of when the data was last successfully synchronized with AWS, assuring staff of data recency.
